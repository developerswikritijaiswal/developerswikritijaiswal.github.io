The Guide to Women’s Clothing Color
Combinations For Fall

By: Pamela Bryant

Fall is the perfect season to add fresh hues to your wardrobe. Fickle weather, ranging from windy to rainy to sunny, makes swapping in appropriate layers fair game, so why not add a little (or a lot of) color too? Whether you’ve already mastered the color wheel and are just looking for a few new tricks, or your closet is swimming in a sea of neutrals and is in need of a heavy dose of brights done right, we’ve got just the thing—six to be precise—to wake up your closet for the season.

Read on for easy tips on how to wear fall colors.

1. LEAN ON COMPLEMENTARY COLORS.

Half the battle is pairing two shades that look really good together. Earth tones are a great place to start, and they match the colors that are naturally found in fall. If you normally wear a lot of brown, try adding green for a subtle nod, or, if you’re ready to hit the ground running, opt for energetic oranges or yellows. Either way, if the colors you picked look harmonious, you’re on the right track.

2. LEAN ON COMPLEMENTARY COLORS.

There are different degrees of wearing pink, but all are up for grabs with the proper execution. A 9-to-5 look is the way to go if you’re unsure of where to start. Pick a patterned button-down shirt that has pink (or coral or a washed-out red) as well as a complementary color such as light blue. Pair your shirt with the usual suspects, and remember to go for slimmer cuts to keep everything looking sharp.

There you have it—your guide to a fall-worthy wardrobe in a few colorful steps. Ask your Stylist to get on the fastest route to your closet refresh.
